This section requires companies to provide the general information about the company. 
* GRI 2: General Disclosures 2021
   * 2-1 Organizational details
      * Legal Name and Ownership
         * What is the organization’s legal name?
            * If the organization use a trading name or business name different from its legal name, what’s the name?
         * Is the organization publicly or privately owned?
         * What is the legal structure of the organization? Is it an incorporated entity, partnership, sole proprietorship, nonprofit, association, or charity?
      * Headquarters and Countries of Operation
         * Where is the headquarters of the organization located?
         * How are the countries of operation for the organization reported?
         * If countries of operation are reported elsewhere, such as in audited financial statements, provide the link
   * 2-2 Entities included in the organization’s sustainability reporting
      * Entities Included in Sustainability Reporting
         * List all entities included in the organization’s sustainability reporting?
         * Are there any differences between the entities listed in the financial reporting and those included in the sustainability reporting?
      * Approach to Consolidating Information
         * How does the organization consolidate information for multiple entities?
         * Does the approach involve adjustments for minority interests? If so, how?
         * How are mergers, acquisitions, and disposals of entities or parts of entities taken into account in the consolidation process?
         * Are there any differences in the consolidation approach across disclosures in this standard and across material topics?
      * Minority Interests
         * Define what constitutes a minority interest in the context of the organization's reporting
         * How does the organization handle minority interests in its reporting process?
   * 2-3 Reporting period, frequency and contact point
      * Reporting Period and Frequency
         * What is the specified reporting period for the sustainability reporting?
         * How frequently the organization conduct sustainability reporting?
      * Alignment with Financial Reporting
         * What is the reporting period for the organization’s financial reporting?
         * Does the reporting period for sustainability reporting align with the organization’s financial reporting?
            * If not, what is the reason for the misalignment?
      * Publication Date
         * When was the report or reported information published?
         * Can the organization specify the exact publication date of the report or information?
      * Contact Point
         * Who is the designated contact point for inquiries about the report or reported information?
         * How can stakeholders reach out to the contact point for questions or clarifications?
   * 2-4 Restatements of information
      * Restatements of Information
         * When and why did the organization make restatements of information from previous reporting periods?
         * What effect did the restatements have on the reported information?
      * Reasons for Restatements
         * What criteria does the organization use to determine when a change or error in previously reported information warrants a restatement?
         * Provide examples of reasons for restatements of information
      * Effect of Restatements
         * How did the restatement impact the previously reported information?
         * If the restatement relates to quantitative information, what is the quantitative change observed?
      * Reporting Non-Restatement
         * If no restatements were made in the reporting period, provide a brief statement confirming this fact
   * 2-5 External assurance
      * Policy and Practice for External Assurance
         * What is the organization's policy for seeking external assurance of its sustainability reporting?
         * How are the highest governance body and senior executives involved in the process of seeking external assurance?
      * External Assurance Report
         * Has the organization's sustainability reporting been externally assured?
         * If externally assured, provide a link or reference to the external assurance report(s) or assurance statement(s).
         * If not externally assured, what are the reasons for not seeking external assurance?
         * Does the report address the following factors:
            * The name of the assurance standards used?
            * The level of assurance obtained?
            * Any limitations of the assurance process?
            * The relationship between the organization and the assurance provider?
      * Additional Information
         * If relevant information is covered in the external assurance reports or statements, is it mentioned?
         * Provide a summary of the scope of information and processes covered in the assurance.
         * What are the responsibilities of the organization relative to the assurance provider?
         * Provide a summary of the opinion or conclusions formally signed off by the assurance provider.
         * Summarize the work performed by the assurance provider.
         * Provide the following information about the assurance provider:
            * The profile of the assurance provider.
            * The level of subject matter expertise of the individuals involved.
   * 2-6 Activities, value chain and other business relationships
      * Sectors of Activity
         * Report the active sector(s)
      * Value Chain Description
         * Describe the organization’s value chain, including:
            * The organization’s activities, products, services, and markets served
            * The organization’s supply chain
            * The entities downstream from the organization and their activities
         * Provide a high-level overview of the value chain
      * Activities in the Value Chain
         * Report significant changes in activities compared to the previous reporting period
      * Products and Services
         * Report the quantity of products or services provided during the reporting period
         * Report the followings if the organization sells products or services that are banned in certain markets or are the subject of stakeholder concerns or public debate
            * The reason for the ban
            * The concerns by stakeholders
            * How the organization has responded to these concerns
      * Markets Served
         * Describe the geographic locations where products and services are offered
         * Describe the demographic or other characteristics of the markets
         * Provide information on the size and relative importance of the markets
      * Supply Chain Relationships
         * Describe significant changes in the structure of the supply chain compared to the previous reporting period
      * Supplier Details
         * Describe the types of suppliers
         * Estimate the number of suppliers throughout its supply chain and in each tier
         * Describe the types of activities related to the organization’s products and services carried out by its suppliers
         * Describe the nature of its business relationships with its suppliers
         * Provide information on the sector-specific characteristics of its supply chain
         * Estimate the monetary value of payments made to its suppliers
         * Describe the geographic location of its suppliers
      * Downstream Entities
         * Describe the types of downstream entities
         * Estimate the number of downstream entities
         * Describe the types of activities related to the organization’s products and services carried out by the downstream entities
         * Describe the nature of its business relationships with the downstream entities
         * Describe the geographic location of the downstream entities
      * Other Business Relationships
         * Describe other relevant business relationships, including types of entities, their activities, and their geographic location
      * Significant Changes
         * Describe significant changes in sectors, value chain, and other business relationships compared to the previous reporting period
   * 2-7 Employees
      * Total Number of Employees
         * Report the total number of employees, and a breakdown of this total by gender and by region
      * Breakdown of Employee Types
         * Permanent employees, and a breakdown by gender and by region
         * Temporary employees, and a breakdown by gender and by region
         * Non-guaranteed hours employees, and a breakdown by gender and by region
         * Full-time employees, and a breakdown by gender and by region
         * Part-time employees, and a breakdown by gender and by region
      * Methodologies and Assumptions
         * Describe the methodologies and assumptions used to compile the data
            * In head count, full-time equivalent (FTE), or using another methodology
            * At the end of the reporting period, as an average across the reporting period, or using another methodology
      * Contextual Information
         * Report contextual information necessary to understand the data reported
      * Significant Fluctuations
         * Describe significant fluctuations in the number of employees during the reporting period and between reporting periods
   * 2-8 Workers who are not employees
      * Number and Type of Non-Employee Workers
         * How many workers who are not employees are engaged by the organization?
         * What types of non-employee workers are engaged (e.g., contractors, temporary agency workers, independent contractors)?
      * Nature of Engagement
         * What is the nature of the engagement for these non-employee workers (e.g., duration, terms of engagement)?
         * In what types of activities or roles are these non-employee workers involved?
      * Geographical Distribution
         * In which geographical locations are these non-employee workers primarily engaged?
      * Significant Changes
         * Have there been any significant changes in the number or type of non-employee workers compared to the previous reporting period?
      * Policies and Practices
         * What policies and practices does the organization have in place to manage and oversee the work of non-employee workers?
         * How does the organization ensure that non-employee workers comply with its standards and requirements?
      * Impact and Integration
         * How are non-employee workers integrated into the organization’s operations and value chain?
         * What impact do non-employee workers have on the organization’s operations and performance?
      * Worker Rights and Conditions
         * How does the organization ensure fair working conditions, rights, and benefits for non-employee workers?
         * Are there any specific measures in place to address the welfare and protection of non-employee workers?
      * Stakeholder Concerns
         * Have there been any stakeholder concerns or issues raised regarding the use of non-employee workers?
         * How has the organization responded to such concerns or issues?
   * 2-9 Governance structure and composition
      * Governance Structure Description
         * Describe the organization’s governance structure, including committees of the highest governance body
      * Committees Responsible for Decision-Making
         * List the committees of the highest governance body that are responsible for decision-making on and overseeing the management of the organization’s impacts on the economy, environment, and people.
      * Composition of the Highest Governance Body and Its Committees
         * Describe the composition of the highest governance body and its committees by
            * Executive and non-executive members
            * Independence
            * Tenure of members on the governance body
            * Number of other significant positions and commitments held by each member, and the nature of the commitments
            * Gender
            * Under-represented social groups
            * Competencies relevant to the impacts of the organization
            * Stakeholder representation
   * 2-10 Nomination and selection of the highest governance body
      * Nomination and Selection Processes Description
         * Describe the nomination and selection processes for the highest governance body and the organization’s committees
      * Criteria for Nominating and Selecting Governance Body Members
         * Describe the criteria used for nominating and selecting highest governance body members, including whether and how the following are taken into consideration
            * Views of stakeholders (including shareholders)
            * Diversity
            * Independence
            * Competencies relevant to the impacts of the organization
   * 2-11 Chair of the highest governance body
      * Chair's Dual Role Report
         * Report whether the chair of the highest governance body is also a senior executive in the organization
      * Explanation of Dual Role (If Applicable)
         * If the chair is also a senior executive, explain their function within the organization’s management, the reasons for this arrangement, and how conflicts of interest are prevented and mitigated
   * 2-12 Role of the highest governance body in overseeing the management of impacts
      * Role in Developing and Updating Organizational Statements and Strategies
         * What is the role of the highest governance body in developing, approving, and updating the followings? 
            * organization’s purpose, value, or mission statements
            * organization’s strategies, policies, and goals related to sustainable development
         * What is the role of senior executives in developing, approving, and updating the followings?
            * organization’s purpose, value, or mission statements
            * organization’s strategies, policies, and goals related to sustainable development
      * Oversight of Due Diligence and Impact Management Processes
         * What is the role of the highest governance body in overseeing the organization’s due diligence and other processes to identify and manage impacts on the economy, environment, and people?
         * How does the highest governance body engage with stakeholders to support these processes?
         * How does the highest governance body consider the outcomes of these processes?
      * Reviewing Effectiveness of Processes
         * What is the role of the highest governance body in reviewing the effectiveness of the organization’s due diligence and impact management processes?
         * How frequently does the highest governance body review the effectiveness of these processes?
      * Stakeholder Engagement
         * How does the highest governance body engage with stakeholders to support due diligence and impact management processes?
         * What is the frequency and means of engagement between the highest governance body and stakeholders?
         * If stakeholder engagement is delegated, to whom is it delegated, and how is the feedback received provided to the highest governance body?
   * 2-13 Delegation of responsibility for managing impacts
      * Delegation of Responsibility
         * How does the highest governance body delegate responsibility for managing the organization’s impacts on the economy, environment, and people?
      * Appointment of Senior Executives
         * Has the highest governance body appointed any senior executives with responsibility for managing impacts?
            * If yes, who are the senior executives appointed and what are their specific responsibilities?
      * Delegation to Other Employees
         * Has the highest governance body delegated responsibility for managing impacts to other employees?
            * If yes, who are the employees delegated and what are their specific responsibilities?
      * Reporting Process and Frequency
         * What is the process for senior executives or other employees to report back to the highest governance body on the management of the organization’s impacts?
         * How frequently do senior executives or other employees report back to the highest governance body on these matters?
   * 2-14 Role of the highest governance body in sustainability reporting
      * Review and Approval Responsibility
         * Does the highest governance body review and approve the reported information, including the organization’s material topics?
            * If yes, describe the process for reviewing and approving the information
      * Explanation for Non-Involvement
         * If the highest governance body is not responsible for reviewing and approving the reported information, including the organization’s material topics, explain the reason for this
      * Establishment of Sustainability Reporting Committee
         * Has the highest governance body established a sustainability reporting committee to support the review and approval process?
      * Adequacy of Internal Controls
         * Does the highest governance body review the adequacy of the organization’s internal controls to strengthen the integrity and credibility of the organization’s sustainability reporting?
         * The involvement of the highest governance body and senior executives in developing the organization’s policy and practice for seeking external assurance is reported under Disclosure 2-5 in this Standard.
   * 2-15 Conflicts of interest
      * Processes for Conflict Prevention and Mitigation
         * Describe the processes for the highest governance body to ensure that conflicts of interest are prevented and mitigated.
      * Disclosure of Conflicts to Stakeholders
         * Are conflicts of interest disclosed to stakeholders?
            * If yes, report on conflicts of interest relating to:
               * Cross-board membership
               * Cross-shareholding with suppliers and other stakeholders
               * Existence of controlling shareholders
               * Related parties, their relationships, transactions, and outstanding balances
   * 2-16 Communication of critical concerns
      * Communication of Critical Concerns
         * Describe whether and how critical concerns are communicated to the highest governance body
      * Reporting Critical Concerns
         * Report the total number and the nature of critical concerns that were communicated to the highest governance body during the reporting period
   * 2-17 Collective knowledge of the highest governance body
      * Measures to Advance Collective Knowledge
         * Report measures taken to advance the collective knowledge, skills, and experience of the highest governance body on sustainable development.
   * 2-18 Evaluation of the performance of the highest governance body
      * Describe the processes for evaluating the performance of the highest governance body in overseeing the management of the organization’s impacts on the economy, environment, and people
      * Report whether the evaluations are independent or not, and the frequency of the evaluations
      * Describe actions taken in response to the evaluations, including changes to the composition of the highest governance body and organizational practices
   * 2-19 Remuneration policies
      * Remuneration Policy Overview
         * What is the organization’s remuneration policy?
         * What are the main objectives and principles of the remuneration policy?
      * Components of Remuneration
         * What are the components of the remuneration for members of the highest governance body and senior executives?
         * How is the remuneration structured for other employees?
      * Performance Metrics and Incentives
         * What performance metrics are used to determine variable components of remuneration?
         * How are these performance metrics linked to the organization’s economic, environmental, and social objectives?
      * Governance and Approval
         * Who is responsible for developing and approving the remuneration policy?
         * What role does the highest governance body play in overseeing and approving remuneration policies and practices?
      * Stakeholder Considerations
         * How does the organization consider stakeholder views in developing and implementing remuneration policies?
      * Disclosure of Remuneration
         * How does the organization disclose information about the remuneration of members of the highest governance body and senior executives?
         * What level of detail is provided in the disclosures regarding the remuneration of the highest governance body and senior executives?
      * Comparative and Benchmarking Practices
         * Does the organization use benchmarking or comparative data in setting remuneration levels?
         * How is the remuneration policy aligned with industry standards and best practices?
      * Frequency of Review
         * How frequently is the remuneration policy reviewed and updated?
      * Fair and Equitable Remuneration
         * What measures are in place to ensure fair and equitable remuneration across the organization?
         * How does the organization address pay gaps and ensure non-discrimination in its remuneration practices?
      * Impact of Remuneration Policies
         * How do remuneration policies impact the organization’s ability to attract and retain talent?
         * What impact do remuneration policies have on employee motivation and organizational performance?
   * 2-20 Process to determine remuneration
      * Describe the process for designing its remuneration policies and for determining remuneration, including:
         * Whether independent highest governance body members or an independent remuneration committee oversees the process for determining remuneration.
         * How the views of stakeholders (including shareholders) regarding remuneration are sought and taken into consideration.
         * Whether remuneration consultants are involved in determining remuneration and, if so, whether they are independent of the organization, its highest governance body, and senior executives.
      * Report the results of votes of stakeholders (including shareholders) on remuneration policies and proposals, if applicable.
   * 2-21 Annual total compensation ratio
      * Disclosure of Compensation Ratios
         * What is the annual total compensation ratio between the highest-paid individual and the median compensation of all employees (excluding the highest-paid individual)?
         * What is the annual total compensation ratio between the highest-paid individual and the median compensation of all employees (including the highest-paid individual)?
      * Calculation Methodology
         * How is the median annual total compensation of all employees calculated?
         * How is the annual total compensation of the highest-paid individual calculated?
         * What components of compensation are included in these calculations (e.g., salary, bonuses, benefits, stock options)?
      * Changes in Compensation Ratios
         * How have the compensation ratios changed compared to the previous reporting period?
         * What factors contributed to any changes in the compensation ratios?
      * Contextual Information
         * How does the organization contextualize the compensation ratios within its industry or sector?
         * How do the compensation ratios reflect the organization’s pay practices and policies?
      * Impact and Implications
         * What impact do the compensation ratios have on employee morale and motivation?
         * How does the organization use the compensation ratio data to inform its remuneration policies and practices?
      * Communication and Transparency
         * How does the organization communicate information about compensation ratios to its stakeholders?
         * What level of transparency is provided regarding the methodology and data used in calculating the compensation ratios?
   * 2-22 Statement on sustainable development strategy
      * Content of the Statement
         * What is the organization's statement on its sustainable development strategy?
         * What are the key elements included in the statement?
      * Alignment with Organizational Goals
         * How does the sustainable development strategy align with the organization's overall goals and objectives?
         * How does it contribute to the organization's mission, vision, and values?
      * Stakeholder Engagement
         * How were stakeholders engaged in the development of the sustainable development strategy?
         * What feedback or input did stakeholders provide?
      * Goals and Targets
         * What specific goals and targets are outlined in the sustainable development strategy?
         * Are these goals measurable, time-bound, and aligned with sustainability principles?
      * Integration into Operations
         * How is the sustainable development strategy integrated into the organization's daily operations and decision-making processes?
         * What mechanisms are in place to ensure implementation across all levels of the organization?
      * Monitoring and Reporting
         * How does the organization monitor progress towards the goals and targets outlined in the sustainable development strategy?
         * What reporting mechanisms are in place to communicate progress to stakeholders?
      * Continuous Improvement
         * How does the organization evaluate the effectiveness of its sustainable development strategy?
         * What processes are in place for continuous improvement and adaptation of the strategy?
      * Transparency and Communication
         * How does the organization communicate its sustainable development strategy to internal and external stakeholders?
         * What level of transparency is provided regarding the strategy's development, implementation, and outcomes?
   * 2-23 Policy commitments
      * Description of Policy Commitments
         * What are the organization's policy commitments related to sustainable development?
         * What specific areas or topics do these policy commitments address?
      * Development Process
         * How were these policy commitments developed?
         * Were stakeholders involved in the development process, and if so, how?
      * Alignment with Standards and Regulations
         * How do the policy commitments align with relevant international standards, guidelines, or regulations?
         * Are there any specific legal or industry requirements that these policy commitments address?
      * Scope and Applicability
         * What is the scope of these policy commitments?
         * To whom do these policy commitments apply (e.g., employees, suppliers, partners)?
      * Implementation Plan
         * What is the organization's plan for implementing these policy commitments?
         * Are there specific actions, targets, or timelines outlined for implementation?
      * Integration into Operations
         * How are these policy commitments integrated into the organization's day-to-day operations?
         * What mechanisms are in place to ensure compliance with these commitments?
      * Monitoring and Evaluation
         * How does the organization monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of its policy commitments?
         * Are there performance indicators or metrics used to track progress?
      * Communication and Transparency
         * How does the organization communicate its policy commitments to internal and external stakeholders?
         * What level of transparency is provided regarding the development, implementation, and outcomes of these commitments?
      * Continuous Improvement
         * How does the organization ensure that its policy commitments remain relevant and effective over time?
         * Is there a process for reviewing and updating these commitments as needed?
   * 2-24 Embedding policy commitments
      * Integration into Governance Structure
         * How are the organization's policy commitments integrated into its governance structure?
         * What mechanisms are in place to ensure that policy commitments are reflected in governance decisions and processes?
      * Leadership and Accountability
         * What roles do senior executives and the highest governance body play in embedding policy commitments?
         * How is accountability established for ensuring the integration of policy commitments throughout the organization?
      * Training and Awareness
         * What training programs or initiatives are in place to raise awareness of policy commitments among employees?
         * How are employees educated about their roles and responsibilities in upholding policy commitments?
      * Policies and Procedures
         * How are policy commitments reflected in the organization's policies, procedures, and guidelines?
         * Are there specific policies or procedures established to support the implementation of policy commitments?
      * Performance Management
         * How are employees' performance evaluated with respect to their adherence to policy commitments?
         * Are there performance indicators or metrics used to track progress in embedding policy commitments?
      * Communication and Engagement
         * How does the organization communicate its policy commitments to employees and stakeholders?
         * What mechanisms are in place to solicit feedback and engagement from stakeholders regarding policy commitments?
      * Supply Chain Integration
         * How are policy commitments extended to the organization's supply chain and business partners?
         * What measures are taken to ensure that suppliers and partners align with the organization's policy commitments?
      * Monitoring and Review
         * How does the organization monitor and review the effectiveness of efforts to embed policy commitments?
         * Are there regular assessments or audits conducted to evaluate progress and identify areas for improvement?
      * Continuous Improvement
         * How does the organization ensure that policy commitments are continuously embedded and strengthened over time?
         * Are there mechanisms in place for reviewing and updating embedding practices as needed?
   * 2-25 Processes to remediate negative impacts
      * Identification of Negative Impacts
         * How does the organization identify negative impacts on the economy, environment, and people resulting from its operations?
      * Establishment of Remediation Processes
         * What processes or mechanisms are in place to remediate negative impacts once they are identified?
         * Are there specific protocols or procedures established for addressing different types of negative impacts?
      * Responsibility and Accountability
         * Who within the organization is responsible for overseeing the remediation of negative impacts?
         * How is accountability assigned for ensuring that remediation processes are effectively implemented?
      * Engagement with Stakeholders
         * How does the organization engage with affected stakeholders in the remediation process?
         * What mechanisms are in place to solicit feedback and input from stakeholders on proposed remediation measures?
      * Collaboration with Partners
         * Does the organization collaborate with external partners or stakeholders to address negative impacts?
         * How are partnerships leveraged to enhance the effectiveness of remediation efforts?
      * Implementation of Remediation Measures
         * How are remediation measures implemented once they are identified and approved?
         * Are there specific timelines or milestones established for the implementation of remediation actions?
      * Monitoring and Evaluation
         * How does the organization monitor the progress and effectiveness of remediation efforts?
         * Are there performance indicators or metrics used to track the impact of remediation measures over time?
      * Communication and Transparency
         * How does the organization communicate its remediation efforts to internal and external stakeholders?
         * What level of transparency is provided regarding the outcomes of remediation processes?
      * Continuous Improvement
         * How does the organization ensure that its processes for remediating negative impacts are continuously reviewed and improved?
         * Are lessons learned from past remediation efforts incorporated into future practices?
   * 2-26 Mechanisms for seeking advice and raising concerns
      * Mechanisms Description
         * Describe the mechanisms for individuals to seek advice on implementing the organization’s policies and practices for responsible business conduct
         * Describe the mechanisms for individuals to raise concerns about the organization’s business conduct
      * Mechanism Operation
         * How do the mechanisms operate, and which level or function in the organization is assigned responsibility for them?
         * Are the mechanisms operated independently of the organization, such as by a third party?
         * What is the process through which concerns are investigated?
         * Are requests for advice and concerns raised treated confidentially?
         * Can the mechanisms be used anonymously?
         * Are intended users informed about the mechanisms, and are they trained on how to use them?
         * What is the accessibility of the mechanisms, such as their availability and language options?
      * Effectiveness and Satisfaction
         * How does the organization ensure it respects users’ human rights and protects them against reprisals for raising concerns?
         * What is the satisfaction level of users with the mechanisms and their outcomes?
         * How many requests for advice were received during the reporting period, and what percentage were answered?
         * How many concerns were raised during the reporting period, and what percentage were addressed, resolved, or found to be unsubstantiated?
   * 2-27 Compliance with laws and regulations
      * Instances of Non-Compliance
         * How many significant instances of non-compliance with laws and regulations occurred during the reporting period?
         * Can you provide a breakdown of these instances by those incurring fines and those resulting in non-monetary sanctions?
      * Fines Incurred
         * What was the total number and monetary value of fines paid for instances of non-compliance during the reporting period?
         * Can you further break down these fines by those incurred in the current reporting period and those from previous periods?
      * Description of Instances
         * Could you describe the significant instances of non-compliance with laws and regulations?
         * How did the organization determine which instances of non-compliance were significant?
      * Guidance on Descriptions
         * Can you specify the geographic location and nature of the matters related to significant instances of non-compliance?
         * Were any of these significant instances repeated or recurring?
      * Laws and Regulations Covered
         * Which laws, regulations, and agreements were considered in assessing compliance?
         * Did the organization include international declarations, conventions, treaties, and voluntary agreements in its compliance assessment?
      * Severity Assessment
         * How did the organization assess the severity of the impact resulting from instances of non-compliance?
         * Were there any external benchmarks used in the sector to determine significant instances of non-compliance?
   * 2-28 Membership associations
      * Identification of Participating Organizations
         * Which industry associations, membership associations, and advocacy organizations does the organization participate in significantly?
         * How does the organization define a "significant role" within these organizations?
      * Criteria for Significance
         * What criteria does the organization use to determine if its role in an association or advocacy organization is significant?
         * Does the organization consider holding positions in governance bodies, participation in projects or committees, or providing substantial funding as factors indicating significance?
      * Types of Participation
         * What types of roles does the organization hold within these associations or organizations (e.g., governance positions, committee memberships, project participation)?
      * Financial Contributions
         * Does the organization provide substantive funding beyond routine membership dues to any of these associations or organizations?
         * How does the organization define "substantive funding"?
      * Strategic Importance
         * How does the organization determine if its membership in an association or organization is strategic to influencing its mission or objectives?
         * In what ways does the organization perceive its membership as critical to its own activities?
      * Transparency and Reporting
         * How does the organization report its participation in these associations or organizations to stakeholders?
         * What level of transparency does the organization provide regarding its roles, contributions, and strategic considerations within these entities?
      * Impact Assessment
         * How does the organization assess the impact of its participation in these associations or organizations on its operations, objectives, and strategic goals?
         * Are there mechanisms in place for evaluating the effectiveness or outcomes of this participation?
      * Continuous Evaluation
         * Does the organization regularly review its participation in these associations or organizations to ensure alignment with its goals and objectives?
         * Are there processes for adjusting or terminating participation based on changing circumstances or priorities?
   * 2-29 Approach to stakeholder engagement
      * Stakeholder Identification and Purpose of Engagement
         * What are the categories of stakeholders engaged by the organization, and how does it identify them?
         * What is the purpose behind the organization's stakeholder engagement efforts?
      * Methods and Frequency of Engagement
         * What types of stakeholder engagement does the organization undertake, and how frequently?
         * When does the organization directly engage with stakeholders, and when does it utilize representatives or other expert resources? Why?
         * Where do stakeholder engagement activities occur, and how is information centralized?
         * What resources, whether financial or human, does the organization allocate to stakeholder engagement?
      * Addressing Barriers and Ensuring Meaningful Engagement
         * How does the organization address potential barriers to stakeholder engagement, such as language and cultural differences?
         * How does the organization engage with at-risk or vulnerable groups, and what special approaches are taken?
         * How does the organization provide stakeholders with understandable and accessible information?
         * How is stakeholder feedback recorded and integrated into decision-making processes, and how are stakeholders informed about the impact of their feedback?
         * How does the organization ensure that it respects the human rights of all stakeholders engaged?
         * How does the organization collaborate with business partners to engage with stakeholders effectively, and what expectations does it set for business partners?
   * 2-30 Collective bargaining agreements
      * Percentage of Employees Covered by Collective Bargaining Agreements
         * What percentage of the organization's total employees are covered by collective bargaining agreements?
         * How is this percentage calculated, and what employees are considered covered by these agreements?
      * Influence of Collective Bargaining Agreements on Working Conditions
         * For employees not covered by collective bargaining agreements, how are their working conditions and terms of employment determined?
         * Does the organization base the working conditions and terms of employment for non-covered employees on agreements that cover other employees or agreements from other organizations? If so, how?
      * Additional Information and Comparisons
         * Can the organization provide a breakdown of the percentage of employees covered by collective bargaining agreements by region?
         * Is the organization able to compare its percentage of employees covered by collective bargaining agreements with industry benchmarks? If so, what are the comparisons?
Section 1-2: Materiality Assessment
Base Logistics


To determine material topics for reporting in accordance with GRI Standards, an organization should follow a four-step process, using the applicable GRI Sector Standards for guidance. 


  



Step 1: Understand the organization’s context: 
In this step, the organization creates an initial high-level overview of its activities, business relationships, sustainability context, and stakeholders.


1. Get the Big Picture:
   1. Know what the organization does and its relationships.
   2. Understand the environment where it operates and who is affected.
   3. Identify stakeholders, including those indirectly impacted.
   4. Consider all business entities under its control or interest.
2. Consider Activities and Relationships:
   1. Evaluate business relationships and activities.
   2. Look at purpose, business model, products/services, and markets.
   3. Understand workforce demographics and types of workers.
   4. Analyze different types of business relationships.
3. Understand Sustainability Context:
   1. Assess economic, environmental, and societal challenges.
   2. Consider compliance with relevant international instruments and laws.
   3. Use GRI Sector Standards for sector-specific insights.
4. Engage Stakeholders:
   1. Identify and involve stakeholders to understand their concerns.
   2. Address barriers to effective engagement.
   3. Respect stakeholders' human rights and protect against reprisals.
Step 2: Identify actual and potential impacts 


In this step, the organization identifies its actual and potential impacts on the economy, environment, and people, including human rights, across its activities and business relationships. 


1. Identify Impacts:
   1. Assess both actual (already occurred) and potential (could occur) impacts on the economy, environment, and people, including human rights.
   2. Include all types: 
      1. negative
      2. positive
      3. short-term
      4. long-term
      5. intended
      6. unintended
      7. reversible
      8. irreversible.
2. Sources of Information:
   1. Use internal and third-party assessments, legal reviews, compliance systems, audits, health and safety inspections, grievance mechanisms, and enterprise risk management systems.
   2. Consult external sources like news organizations and civil society groups.
3. Stakeholder Engagement:
   1. Engage directly with stakeholders, removing barriers like language and cultural differences.
   2. Use specific approaches for vulnerable groups and respect their rights.
4. Ongoing Assessment:
   1. Regularly review impacts as the organization’s activities and context change.
   2. Focus first on identifying negative impacts, especially if resources are limited.
5. Context Consideration:
   1. Refer to GRI Sector Standards for typical impacts.
   2. Use initial scoping for large or diverse operations to identify significant impact areas.


Ensure documentation of changes and the rationale behind them for transparency.


Step 3: Assess the significance of the impacts
Evaluate the importance of the identified impacts by considering their significance and consulting the Sector Standards.


1. Prioritize Impacts:
   1. Evaluate the significance of identified impacts to focus on the most important ones for action and reporting.
   2. Consider both quantitative and qualitative factors, including severity and likelihood.
2. Severity Assessment:
   1. Severity is determined by the scale, scope, and irremediable character of negative impacts.
   2. Severity should be compared within the organization's impacts, not globally.
3. Likelihood Assessment:
   1. Likelihood measures the chance of potential negative impacts happening.
   2. Prioritize highly severe impacts, even if they are less likely to occur.
4. Positive Impact Assessment:
   1. Positive impacts are assessed based on followings:
      1. scale
      2. scope
      3. likelihood.
   2. They cannot offset negative impacts.


Step 4: Determine material topics for reporting


Prioritize the most significant impacts for reporting based on the previous steps.


1. Set Material Topic Threshold:
   1. Arrange impacts by significance and set a threshold for reporting.
   2. Material topics are determined solely by impact significance.
2. Group Impacts into Topics:
   1. Group impacts based on followings:
      1. business activities
      2. stakeholder categories
      3. resource use.
   2. Test the selection against GRI Sector Standards and consult experts for validation.
3. Approval and Reporting:
   1. Highest governance body or senior executives approve material topics.
   2. Document reasons for excluding any potential material topics.
   3. Determine what to report for each material topic and ensure transparency in the process.
   
GENERAL
* GRI 3: Material Topics 2021
   * 3-1 Process to determine material topics
      * How were material topics identified?
         * What methods were used to pinpoint significant areas of impact?
         * Where did the organization gather information, and from whom?
         * How broad or narrow was the focus, and were any areas excluded?
      * How were impacts prioritized for reporting?
         * How did the organization measure the importance of each impact?
            * For negative impacts, what made them severe and likely to happen?
            * For positive impacts, what determined their significance?
         * Did the organization have a specific approach to sorting impacts?
            * how was the threshold for reporting determined?
         * Did the top leadership review and approve these significant areas?
      * Who informed the process of determining material topics?
         * Who participated in identifying and assessing these significant areas?
         * How did input from stakeholders and experts shape the identification and assessment process?
         * Were there any disagreements among stakeholders, and if so, how were they resolved?
   * 3-2 List of material topics
      * List of Material Topics:
         * What are the material topics identified by the organization?
         * How are these material topics categorized or grouped to effectively communicate their impacts?
      * Changes to the List of Material Topics:
         * Has there been any change to the list of material topics compared to the previous reporting period?
            * If yes, why has a topic previously considered material been deemed not material anymore, or why has a new topic been identified as material for the current reporting period?
